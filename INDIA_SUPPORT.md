## Understanding Your Results

### AQI Ranges (India-Specific)
- **0-50**: Good (Rare in metro cities)
- **51-100**: Satisfactory (Occasional in coastal cities)
- **101-200**: Moderate (Common year-round)
- **201-300**: Poor (Delhi winters, Diwali period)
- **301-400**: Very Poor (Severe pollution episodes)
- **401-500**: Severe (Emergency conditions)

### What the AI Checks in Your Video

#### Indoor Issues (Common in Indian Homes)
- **Ventilation**: Windows closed due to outdoor pollution
- **AC Units**: Overuse without fresh air intake
- **Kitchen Smoke**: Cooking without exhaust (masala, frying)
- **Incense/Agarbatti**: Daily puja smoke accumulation
- **Dust**: Construction activity, unpaved roads nearby
- **Humidity**: Monsoon dampness, mold growth

#### Outdoor Context (Indian Cities)
- High vehicular pollution
- Construction dust
- Industrial emissions
- Seasonal crop burning (North India)
- Festival fireworks (Diwali)
- Summer heat and dust storms

## Tips for Indian Users

### Best Times to Check AQI
- **Morning**: 6-8 AM (before traffic peaks)
- **Evening**: 6-9 PM (high pollution hours)
- **Post-Rain**: Usually improved AQI
- **Winter Mornings**: Worst AQI in North India

### Seasonal Considerations
- **Summer** (Mar-Jun): Dust storms, high temperature
- **Monsoon** (Jul-Sep): High humidity, mold risk
- **Post-Monsoon** (Oct-Nov): Stubble burning, Diwali
- **Winter** (Dec-Feb): Severe AQI in North, fog traps pollutants

### Room Improvements for Indian Climate
1. **Use HEPA Air Purifiers** (PM2.5 removal)
2. **Cross-Ventilation** (early morning when AQI is better)
3. **Indoor Plants** (Money plant, Areca palm, Snake plant)
4. **Kitchen Exhaust** (mandatory for Indian cooking)
5. **Dehumidifiers** (coastal/monsoon regions)
6. **Door Seals** (prevent outdoor pollution entry)
